Need Help with a Wild Animal?

We are here to assist you 24/7

Have You Found an Orphaned or Injured Wild Animal?

In this situation, you need to locate the closest state-licensed wildlife rehabilitator near you who works with the species.

Note: Personally, I rehabilitate New York-native predator-type mammals (foxes, minks, weasels). However, I cannot always intake an animal, as I am a one-man show with wildlife rehab, and I have capacity capacity limitations.

Animal Help Now Website

The Animal Help Now (AHN) website has been a GREAT resource for help connect the public and rehabbers. I really like this tool, as it tends to have the most up-to-date information of any wildlife help search engine I know of.

How the AHN Site Works:

  1. Type in your address.
  2. It will populate a list of rehabbers based on that location.
  3. The search results will state which species each rehabber works with.

Note: It is important that not all wildlife rehabilitators work with-or are licensed for-all native species.

Temporary Care Instructions

In the meantime, if safely possible, keep the animal contained in a quiet, dark area with minimal stimulation and human/pet traffic. Do not handle it. Avoid attempting to feed the animal until it gets to a rehabber, as it can sometimes do more harm than good if the animal is not yet stabilized and hydrated.

Do You Need Help with a Captive-Bred Exotic?

Need placement for a captive-born fox, wolfdog, or other exotic pet or rescue? These animals must stay in captivity, as it is both illegal and immoral to release a captive-bred wild animal. Please feel free to send me a detailed email if you need assistance with the following information:

  • Species
  • Age
  • Vet history (if any)
  • Temperament
  • Important details or quirks
  • Photos of the animal

Note: Depending on the species and my capacity, I cannot guarantee that I can intake your animal.

Wolfdog/Wolf Hybrid Placement

Many wolfdog sanctuaries are always at full capacity. I strongly suggest posting on wolfdog rehoming groups on Facebook. Request to join these groups before posting. If a facility or qualified home is looking for a specific temperament, gender, content %, etc., they will be watching these group postings. Use your discretion when vetting potential homes.
